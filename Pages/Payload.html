UAV Payload
Mechanism

An amazing university project,
Simple, robust and reliable UAV payload system.

Background Story

This is a project that I did as part of a larger project on an autonomous UAV. The UAV project was about designing an autonomous system that could take off from a designated spot and hover around a perimeter to find the marked spots and drop the payload.
The main UAV was developed based on a Raspberry Pi running Linux. We had two team members working on the navigation using ROS (Robot Operating System). The third member worked on image processing and detection of the marked spots on the ground. The main frame was developed by another team member and finally, a project manager coordinated all the tasks with us.
This unit was delivered in two semesters and I joined the team in the second semester and the team assigned the payload system to me.

Key Features

The payload mechanism is required to drop the load once the target is detected. The team suggested having a servo motor that could be triggered by the main flight controller. The load was a cylindrical piece of wood that needed to drop on the marked spot. I designed the payload mechanism to hold the load and release it once the servo motor is triggered. The structure of the design could hold the wood in place using the attached arm on the shaft of the servo motor. For simplicity, I designed a recessed box to secure the servo motor on the main structure.

Challenges and Solutions

Before attempting to design the payload mechanism, the project manager discussed the main requirements for this design as well as extra features that could improve the stability of the cylindrical structure.
Due to the high airflow around the payload mechanism accuracy of the drop could be affected. One of the assessment criteria was the precision of the drop in the project. I decided to have some sort of free suspension in my design to prevent any undesired movement during the flight.
Using some small bearings, I was able to create two angles of freedom to keep the payload mechanism straight and perpendicular to the ground. This feature would significantly improve the payload release.
The payload system is required to be light and stable in high winds. The light design of the payload created some complexity and I had issues with balancing the structure on the ground. Due to the unequal weight of printed PLA on two sides of the cylinder (One side of the cylinder was heavier due to the servo motor mounting), the payload mechanism wouldn't stay perpendicular to the ground and I needed to find the centre of gravity and attempt to move it to the middle of the cylinder. I managed to do so by adjusting the location of the bearings. The following videos show how the suspension mechanism works. The stability of the cylinder was further improved when loaded.



Final Words

This university project was one of my favorite projects and I learned more about 3D printing and how to design efficiently to meet the project requirements.
Our team received the full mark for this project and my 3D design was recognised as the best payload mechanism among all other groups.
