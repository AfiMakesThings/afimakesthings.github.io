Mini CNC Plotter

This project is inspired by a YouTube video
to create a plotter from a broken CD driver.


Background Story

I love watching YouTube especially technical videos about DIY projects and how people can do interesting and useful projects using spare parts from their labs.
I found this project on YouTube and after watching the video, I realized that I have all the mechanical parts of it so I decided to make this plotter using the parts from my lab. (Apologies for the low-quality images in this page)

Key Features

The main features of this project are simplicity and low cost. I used some metal pieces and screws to secure the pen on the Z-axis assembly however by adjusting the screws I could use a marker instead of a pen.
The majority of parts are salvaged from two broken CD drivers such as stepper motors, slides and the mainframes. Any new drawing could be converted to GRBL (G-code) using a G-code generator to be used by the plotter. The effective area of the plotter is roughly 50X50 mm.

Challenges and Solutions

This design was the first complex project for me and I did not know how to design parts as components and join all of them together in the end. This added so much more complexity to my design because any small change would affect the entire design.
The rear bumper was one of the few curved shapes that I managed to design it in one piece and combine with the existing part. For this purpose, I used the Form feature of the Fusion360 that allows me to manually create and form a curved surface. The following image shows how I connected all the parts.

Final Words

This was an interesting project for me because of used salvaged old CD drives and made a useful mini plotter from them. Working with the small stepper motors and the techniques to drive them helped me to develop a basic understanding of how these motors work.
This project could be further improved or even converted to a laser engraving machine. For this purpose, a DVD writer laser could replace the pen to engrave a surface such as a piece of wood. Laser engraver would have more complexity compared to this project. The laser would need a dedicated constant current source to reliably drive it and keep the power and intensity of the laser beam constant.
