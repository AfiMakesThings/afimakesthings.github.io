Trailer
Guidance System

Jackknifing prevention system with
screen and alarm.


Background Story

This is my first-year project (my master's degree) that I did with three other students. We had two weeks to find a project idea through brainstorming as a team. We found a problem or issue in jackknifing that could severely damage trucks and trailers during reversing.
During the brainstorming meetings, we found several solutions however some of them were more challenging to implement. For instance, an IR transmitter and receiver could be used on trucks and trailers to calculate the angle between them. After an extensive analysis, we identified that dust and moisture could potentially affect the readings and trigger a false alarm.
The below diagram shows the IR sensor that could be used for this purpose.


Eventually, we decided to proceed with a motion sensor to be able to detect the angle between trailers and trucks.

Key Features

Our solution for the jackknifing problem was using two IMUs (Inertial Measurement Unit) and calculating the instantaneous angle by subtracting the angle of the truck from the trailer. I had a bit of experience in using the MPU6050 module with Arduino so I suggested using the same module in this project. This module is a 3-axis accelerometer and gyroscope sensor.
The prevention system was required to have an alarm and indicator to notify the driver if the jackknifing was about to occur. In our project, we decided to have an LCD screen to show the angle while driving and an LED to flash if the angle is over the setpoint. A buzzer would also alarm the driver in this scenario.
Using the push buttons around the screen, the driver could adjust the jackknifing angle manually and set the alarms based on his/her preferences and the specifications of the truck. Any alarm could be activated or deactivated using the in-built menu.

We created a graphical representation of the truck and its trailer to allow the driver to simply understand the reversing angle. The below image shows the triggered alarm during reversing. As shown below, the LED is turned on indicating the unsafe reversing angle.

Challenges and Solutions

This project was designed to be done as a group of four. We were four strangers with different backgrounds trying to deliver a project from the brainstorming stage to the final product. I had a bit more experience with Arduino than others so the team decided to assign me as the project manager.
It was challenging to know each team member's skills capabilities and even their passion and dedication to the project. Other than managing the project, I designed and built the hardware, created the enclosure, and worked a bit on the software. In the context of the hardware design, troubleshooting the board was sometimes challenging because the prototype was made on a prototyping PCB with hand soldering and poor connection between parts of the circuit could cause strange behaviour in the board. I designed the schematics and PCB in a way to be able to mount the IMU sensor separately on any part of the truck and trailer. The following image is a snapshot of the schematic design in Altium.


The below image shows the 3D view of the PCB that I made in Altium.

I designed the enclosure to fit all parts of the circuits inside a box however, we could not get a free timeslot to 3D-print the model. We urgently needed to prepare the hardware for presentation day so we managed to buy an off-the-shelf container and drilled some holes to make it suitable for our project.
The following image is the 3D model of the enclosure that I designed for this project.

Final Words

This was my first practical project in my master's degree. I had a chance to collaborate with other students and achieve a team goal.
Despite all the challenges, I enjoyed developing an idea to solve a real-life problem. This project had a significant difference from my other DIY projects, during this project I learned how to write a comprehensive academic report and capture all the details for reference. In terms of product design and development, there are lots of opportunities that could optimise this project and make it more industrial rather than a simple university project. For instance, the functionality of this product could be added to the existing display of the vehicle without adding an extra display for this purpose.
Overall, this project helped me to develop my teamwork skills and manage a small team to create a functional product as well as control timelines and deliverables.
