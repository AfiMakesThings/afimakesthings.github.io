Humidity Controller

Anti-condensation control circuit,
Simple and reliable.


Background Story

The idea for this project came from one of my friends who needed a circuit to protect his electrical equipment against condensation. I designed the schematic and PCB in less than three hours and ordered from the JLCPCB.
One of the main purposes of this project was to familiarize my friend with easyEDA and design PCBs and order them without any hassle.

Key Features

This board uses a DHT11 sensor to measure relative humidity and temperature. All the measurements are analyzed and evaluated in an Arduino take action based on the captured values. The Arduino Nano can enable or disable two relays to turn on/off a heater and fan. As usual, I have used two female headers in parallel to simply attach the Arduino to the board.
The DHT sensor can be attached to the board using the female header on the board. This will allow the user to easily replace the sensor in case of any issue or problem with the sensor.
Two relay circuits with an LED to indicate the status of the contacts. The free-wheeling diode is also placed across the relay coil to protect the BJT against any undesired voltage spikes from the coil.
There are several Phoenix screw terminals on the board to allow the user to hook up the fan, heater, and power source without any soldering.

Challenges and Solutions

This design was quite simple to create and I needed to make this as quick as possible and show my friend how to use easyEDA for his circuit designs.
The main challenge was managing the time and explaining every stage of the design while trying to focus on the technical aspects of the schematic and PCB.
Other than that, I struggled to find footprints with the correct 3D models of parts. This took a bit of time to find and validate each part however, the final 3D model of the design was quite interesting for my friend because he could somehow see the finalized and manufactured PCB.

Final Words

This was the quickest project that I have ever done. I finished the whole design including brainstorming, schematic and PCB design in less than 3 hours.
My friend and I ordered the PCB from the JLCPCB and he received the boards in two weeks. He was quite satisfied with the quality of the boards.
Eventually, we developed an Arduino code to read the measurements from the DHT11 sensor and activate the heater and fan if needed.
